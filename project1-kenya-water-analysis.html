Executive Summary & Methodology

This analysis examines Kenya's water access and climate resilience trajectory from 2000 to 2023, using publicly available data from the World Bank Development Indicators database, WHO/UNICEF Joint Monitoring Programme (JMP), and Kenya's National Drought Management Authority (NDMA). The study applies trend analysis, county-level disaggregation, and climate vulnerability indexing to assess equity gaps between urban and rural populations, identify high-risk ASAL (Arid and Semi-Arid Land) counties, and evaluate Kenya's progress toward SDG 6 (Clean Water and Sanitation) targets. Findings are intended to inform adaptive programme design and resource allocation for water security interventions.

06 · Key Findings & Recommendations
⚠️
Critical ASAL Access Gap

Northern ASAL counties (Mandera, Wajir, Turkana, Marsabit) remain critically underserved with access rates below 35%, compared to the national average of 73%. This 38+ percentage point gap represents a structural equity failure requiring targeted investment.

📉
Urban–Rural Disparity Persists

Despite national progress, the urban–rural gap remains 40 percentage points (91% vs 51%). Rural water infrastructure investment has not kept pace with population growth, particularly in peri-urban and semi-arid zones.

📈
National Progress is Encouraging

Kenya has achieved a 23 percentage point improvement in national water access since 2000, with notable gains in urban areas. This demonstrates the effectiveness of sustained WASH investment when properly coordinated.

🌡️
Climate Risk Amplifies Vulnerability

Counties with high drought frequency (perennial and recurrent) show the lowest water access rates, confirming that climate vulnerability and infrastructure gaps are compounding factors. Resilience programming must integrate climate-adaptive WASH design.

💧
Sanitation Lags Behind Water Access

Sanitation coverage (48% national) significantly lags behind water access (73%), particularly in rural areas where open defecation remains prevalent. SDG 6.2 targets on sanitation are at risk of being missed.

🎯
Targeted Programming Recommended

Evidence suggests that concentrating WASH investment in the 13 highest-risk counties could address ~60% of the access gap with approximately 30% of the total required budget, offering high return on investment for donor programming.
