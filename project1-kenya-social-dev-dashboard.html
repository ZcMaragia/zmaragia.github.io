Key Findings & Recommendations

Analysis Summary
✅ Positive — Education Progress

Primary school enrolment increased by 8.2 percentage points between 2010–2022, with gender parity at primary level nearly achieved (parity index: 0.97). This reflects the impact of Kenya's Free Day Secondary Education policy.

✅ Positive — Child Health

Under-5 mortality declined sharply from 73.1 to 43.2 per 1,000 live births — a 41% reduction — driven by expanded immunisation coverage, skilled birth attendance, and community health worker programmes.

⚠️ Critical Gap — Arid & Semi-Arid Regions

Counties such as Turkana and Mandera exhibit poverty rates exceeding 79%, with water access below 25%. These regions are severely off track for SDG 1, 3, and 6, requiring targeted, conflict-sensitive programming and resource reallocation.

🔶 Caution — Urban-Rural WASH Gap

Urban water access (87%) far outpaces rural access (47%), despite overall national improvement. Sanitation disparities are even wider. Programmes should prioritise last-mile rural WASH infrastructure to close the equity gap.

💡 Recommendation — Adaptive M&E

County-level disaggregation reveals that national averages mask extreme subnational disparities. Programmes should adopt adaptive management cycles with quarterly county-level data reviews to trigger timely course corrections.

💡 Recommendation — Gender Targeting

While primary gender parity is near-achieved, secondary female enrolment (54.1%) and tertiary participation remain low. Conditional cash transfer programmes and community awareness campaigns targeting girls' retention should be prioritised.
